Explore Career Roles in Media

Molly Howard
Sep 8, 2022

New Media consists of digital content. Traditional media is the foundation for digital content. Print media creates a message, shares information, or even entertains. Media types include more conventional styles such as magazines, billboards, new papers, and comic books. Print materials were the only media content source until technology and social media.

A degree in communications offers a wide range of employment opportunities. Media and communication majors can be found everywhere. With roles ranging from traditional to nontraditional, such as a media manager or radio/tv broadcaster. More traditional roles include public relations, event planning, and telecommunications. The evolving technology makes the major broader as print and paper are only some of the means for media and communication. Due to the rise of technology and social media, new roles emerged. There are new ways to market and advertise. With a degree in media, you will have the skills and knowledge necessary to excel in any career that requires strong communication skills. You will also gain an understanding of how people communicate and how to use tools such as social media and platforms that offer services effectively. With this knowledge, you can create effective campaigns and strategies to help your organization reach its goals.
